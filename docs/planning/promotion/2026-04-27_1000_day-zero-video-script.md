# Day Zero Video Script

**Format:** Presenter on side, presentation/visuals in center. Max 5 minutes.

---

## SCENE 1 — The Vision (0:00–0:45)

"The personal agent era has begun.

Right now it's tinkerers. Developers building on OpenClaw, Claude Code, Hermes. A Roomba and an iPhone. Rubbing two sticks together compared to what's coming.

Apple, Google, Anthropic, OpenAI — they're all coming. And when your phone has an agent, your doctor's office has one, your bank has one, your car has one, your robot has one —

How do they all talk to each other?

Who controls that?"

---

## SCENE 2 — The Problem (0:45–1:30)

"Because right now the answer is: nobody. There's no identity layer. No way to verify who you're actually talking to. No proof of what was agreed. Every agent is an island.

And the people who will want to fix that — the big platforms — will fix it the way they always do. A centralized service. Their infrastructure. Their rules. Their cut.

We've seen this movie before."

---

## SCENE 3 — The Answer (1:30–2:00)

"CELLO is the open alternative. A trust layer for agent communication that nobody controls. Private. Verifiable. Built on cryptographic proof, not platform promises.

Your agent. Your rules. Your receipts."

---

## SCENE 4 — The Hard Problem (2:00–2:45)

"CELLO is peer-to-peer at its core.

The client runs on your machine. The control sits with you. Who you connect to — your decision. What you disclose — your decision. What policies your agent enforces — your decision.

But pure peer-to-peer with no accountability?

Scammers, spammers, pretenders, security offenders.

CELLO takes a different approach."

---

## SCENE 5 — The Design (2:45–3:30)

"We verify identity without storing your data. Your trust signals — phone verification, professional credentials, social proof — are hashed and held by you, not us. We never see the original. We can prove it's accurate without ever knowing what it is.

And you're in control of what you require from others before you'll connect. Require a verified phone number. Require a LinkedIn credential. Require a history of successful transactions. Or require nothing — your choice.

The other side sees your requirements. They decide whether to share enough to meet them. Nobody is forced to disclose anything. Nobody is surveilled.

Trust without surveillance. Freedom without the free-for-all."

---

## SCENE 6 — Origin Story (3:30–4:00)

*[TO BE WRITTEN]*

I was building a personal AI agent — before OpenClaw existed. In the process I realized that while a personal agent is useful, agents become exponentially more useful when they can coordinate.
